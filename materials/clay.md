# Clay
Using clay allows for precise modeling. There are "direct building" and "molding" methods, but here I summarize the general properties of modeling.

|Property|Detail|
|---|---|
|Formability|It's easy to use for expressing details.|
|Weight|Oil clay is heavy, but lightweight clay is as light as polyurethane foam.|
|Hardness|After drying, it hardens and retains its shape, but becomes prone to cracking.|
|Breathability|It has no air bubbles, so it's not breathable. Therefore, a fan must be installed inside.|
|Durability|Oil clay has low durability, but lightweight clay has relatively high durability.|

## Processing Method
### Armature Construction
To prevent collapse due to the weight of the clay, create a frame using aluminum wire.

### Sculpting
Apply clay to the core material and shape it with a spatula or your fingers.

### Molding *In the case of resin casting
Cover the completed prototype with silicone or similar material to create a female mold.

### Sanding
After hardening, polish the surface using sandpaper or a rotary tool.

### Marking
Use a marking needle or spatula to mark the position. This is necessary to accurately determine the position of the left and right parts.
