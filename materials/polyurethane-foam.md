# Polyurethane Foam
Polyurethan foam is the most commonly used material in head production. It's lightweight, making it easy to process, and its durability can be enhanced by adjusting its density.


|Property|Detail|
|---|---|
|Density|The higher the density, the more resistant it is to sagging. the lower the density, the easier it is to process.|
|Hardness|The softer the polyurethane foam, the easier it is to crush. The harder it is, the more difficult it is to process.|
|Breathability|It has a bubble structure, so it's highly breathable.|
|Water absorption|It has a bubble structure, so it's absorbent.|
|Chemical resistance|It's resistant to organic solvents, so organic solvent-based adhesives can be used.|
|Aging|It deteriorates through hydrolysis.|

## Processing Method
### Cutting
Polyurethane foam can be cut using large scissors.

### Adhesion
Apply rubber-based adhesive to both sides of the polyurethane foam and bond them together.

### Shaving
Cut the surface finely with small scissors.

### Marking
Using an oil-based marker, you can easily draw lines there.
